Color palettes · mood boards

Eight palettes mapped across two axes: warm to cool, and soft to bold. Each board pairs the five color roles with shape language and photography direction. Type shown in a neutral system font; clients choose their font pairing separately.

The spectrum

Every palette sits at a coordinate on two axes: temperature (warm to cool) and energy (bold to soft). This is the anchor for the whole system, because these are the two questions anyone can answer about their own brand without design vocabulary. Should it feel more energizing or more calming? Warmer or cooler? Two answers place a client on the map, and the nearest palette is theirs. It also keeps the collection honest: each palette owns its own territory, so no two options compete for the same client, and any future palette has to earn an empty spot rather than crowd an existing one.

Onboarding flow: ask the two questions, mark the answers on the map, and present the one or two nearest palettes as the recommendation. The client chooses between neighbors, never from a lineup of eight.

Working rules

These apply to every palette. The role names stay the same across all eight, so any layout built on the tokens accepts any palette.

Two or three text colorsAnchor for primary, Muted for captions and metadata, Primary for links and emphasis. Nothing else carries words on a light page.
60 / 30 / 10Roughly 60% neutral and tint, 30% anchor, 10% accent. The accents are seasoning. When in doubt, use less of them.
Light and dark tiersContrast only exists between tiers. Light text on a light background never works. Put text on Anchor, or Anchor on light, or use Primary.
Tone-on-tone for depthAccent fill with Anchor text, Secondary fill with Anchor text. Same family, far apart in lightness. The considered, layered look.
Each accent has one jobPrimary carries emphasis and action. Accent highlights. Secondary structures: panels, timelines, checks. Only Primary and Anchor carry meaning.
Meaning in Anchor, mood in accentsAnything that must be read at a glance lives in Anchor, Primary, or Muted, never in a light fill. Light fills are decoration.
Do
  • Pair a Primary heading with Anchor body on the tint.
  • Use an Anchor section with an Accent headline for high energy.
  • Give light-fill cards a hairline border so they read on the tint.
  • Make primary buttons Anchor or Primary, secondary buttons an Accent fill.
  • Use Muted for captions and metadata, not faded Anchor.
Don't
  • Set Accent, Secondary, or Tint as text on a light background.
  • Introduce hues from outside the palette.
  • Rely on the Neutral / Tint difference alone to separate sections.
  • Use more than three text colors in one layout.
  • Let key information live in a light accent fill.
